CAT Quant Topic Weightage

This chart provides an estimated breakdown of how frequently topics appear in the CAT's Quantitative Aptitude section. Use it to guide your study priorities. While Arithmetic and Algebra form the bulk of the exam, a balanced preparation across all topics is key to a high score.

Arithmetic

Arithmetic consistently forms the largest part of the Quant section. A strong foundation here is essential for tackling a significant portion of the exam questions, covering everything from basic calculations to complex problems involving profit, loss, interest, and rates.

Profit = SP – CP

Loss = CP – SP

Profit %: (ProfitCP) × 100

Discount: MP – SP

Simple & Compound Interest

Simple Interest (SI): P × R × T100

Compound Interest (CI): P(1 + R100)T - P

CI-SI Difference (2 yrs): P(R100)2

Time, Speed, and Distance

Distance = Speed × Time

Relative Speed (same): |S1 - S2|

Time and Work

A & B Together: aba+b days

Total Work = Efficiency × Time

Algebra

Algebra is a close second to Arithmetic in terms of question frequency. Mastery of algebraic manipulations, equations, and functions is critical for solving a wide variety of problems that test your logical and abstract reasoning skills.

Geometry & Mensuration

Geometry and Mensuration questions test your spatial reasoning and ability to apply formulas to various shapes. This section covers everything from basic properties of lines and triangles to the volumes and surface areas of 3D objects, requiring both visualization and calculation skills.

Number Systems

The Number Systems topic covers the fundamental properties and relationships of numbers. Questions often test your knowledge of divisibility, factors, multiples, and the structure of number series. A solid grasp of these concepts is crucial for solving logic-based quantitative problems.

HCF and LCM

HCF × LCM = Product of the two numbers

Divisibility Rules: Knowledge for 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9, 11 is vital.

Summation Formulas

Sum of first n numbers: n(n+1)2

Sum of first n squares: n(n+1)(2n+1)6

Sum of first n cubes: (n(n+1)2)2

Modern Mathematics

Modern Math includes topics that require logical reasoning and systematic counting. While having fewer questions compared to Arithmetic or Algebra, these topics can be high-scoring if the fundamental concepts of arrangements, selections, and probability are well understood.

Permutation & Combination

Permutation (Arrangement): nPr = n!(n-r)!

Combination (Selection): nCr = n!(n-r)!r!

Probability

P(Event): Favorable OutcomesTotal Outcomes

P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Set Theory

n(A ∪ B) = n(A) + n(B) - n(A ∩ B)

n(A ∪ B ∪ C) = n(A) + n(B) + n(C) - n(A ∩ B) - n(B ∩ C) - n(C ∩ A) + n(A ∩ B ∩ C)
